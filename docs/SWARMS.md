# BUNK Build Swarms

All BUNK swarms are active for the production build in `carbonactual/omnii`.

## Operating command

ABBA routes work across swarms based on role, task, risk, authority, privacy, cost, provider availability, and required human approval.

No swarm may bypass SEAL, publish unverified claims, release protected records, or approve its own high-impact work.

## Active swarms

### 1. Product and Architecture Swarm
Owns product boundaries, release scope, information architecture, domain decomposition, cross-module contracts, and decision records.

### 2. UX and Design System Swarm
Owns public, customer, partner, and admin experiences; mobile-first interaction; accessibility; loading, empty, error, success, and permission states.

### 3. Property Registry and Marketplace Swarm
Owns properties, buildings, units, listings, wanted requests, discovery, saved properties, publication states, and marketplace operations.

### 4. Search, Maps, and Matching Swarm
Owns text, semantic, geospatial, map, commute-aware, similar-property, affordability, and explainable match services.

### 5. Trust, Verification, Proof, Vault, and SEAL Swarm
Owns ownership claims, authority-to-list, evidence, verification states, protected custody, human approvals, public disclosure, and audit lineage.

### 6. CRM, Agent, Agency, and Affiliate Swarm
Owns leads, sources, assignments, pipeline, inspections, attribution, referrals, commission proof, agency teams, and affiliate controls.

### 7. Inspection and Transaction Swarm
Owns inspection scheduling, routing, check-in, evidence, offers, negotiation, agreements, signatures, handover, and formed transaction states.

### 8. Tenancy and Property Operations Swarm
Owns tenancies, occupants, rent schedules, notices, renewals, arrears, owner statements, management operations, and occupancy history.

### 9. Maintenance and Service Marketplace Swarm
Owns issue intake, severity, responsibility, approvals, vendor assignment, estimates, work orders, completion proof, ratings, and closure.

### 10. Finance, Payments, Ledger, and Reconciliation Swarm
Owns invoices, payment intents, payments, allocations, refunds, commissions, payouts, immutable ledger entries, reconciliation, and I/O interfaces.

### 11. Investment, Rent-to-Own, and Mortgage Swarm
Owns feature-flagged property investment, portfolio, rent-to-own, affordability, regulated partner matching, disclosures, and compliance gates.

### 12. ABBA, AI Agents, and Provider Routing Swarm
Owns BUNK agents, structured outputs, tool authorization, prompt versioning, confidence, evidence, escalation, cost tracking, and provider routing.

### 13. Risk, Redress, Compliance, and Privacy Swarm
Owns fraud signals, duplicate detection, suspicious pricing, disputes, evidence preservation, complaint handling, country rules, privacy requests, retention, and regulatory gates.

### 14. Data, Supabase, Events, Analytics, and Observability Swarm
Owns schema, migrations, PostGIS, RLS, event contracts, Pulse, Actual records, ATLAS publications, analytics, audit logs, health checks, and telemetry.

### 15. DevOps, Security, Testing, and Release Swarm
Owns CI, preview environments, staging, production, secrets, backups, dependency security, tests, load checks, release gates, rollback, and operational readiness.

### 16. Growth, Supply, Demand, Content, and Launch Swarm
Owns verified supply acquisition, demand generation, SEO, referrals, institution partnerships, market education, pricing, monetization experiments, and launch operations.

## First coordinated objective

Deliver the first production vertical slice:

Human onboarding
→ role resolution
→ property creation
→ ownership or authority evidence
→ human verification review
→ listing publication
→ public search
→ save
→ inspection booking
→ inspection completion
→ Proof and Pulse

## Required swarm output

Each swarm must produce:

- owned modules
- interfaces and contracts
- database entities
- API routes
- risks and controls
- tests
- operational metrics
- dependencies
- release criteria

## Global completion rule

A swarm task is not complete until the route is implemented, tested, permissioned, observable, documented, and connected to Proof, Pulse, and human authority where required.
